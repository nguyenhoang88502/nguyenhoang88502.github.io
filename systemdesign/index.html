“Việc” — The Ultimate Co-Working Space Cafe

Remote work and flexible study environments have become essential for students and freelancers. However, traditional cafes are often noisy and lack privacy, while professional co-working spaces tend to be expensive. The “Việc” co-working cafe was designed as a systems engineering solution to bridge this gap by combining workspace productivity with an affordable coffee shop environment.

Project Report Presentation Slides

Project Context

The project targets the National University Village in Thu Duc, Ho Chi Minh City — an area populated by more than 200,000 students and young professionals. The concept was developed as a scalable business system that integrates workspace design, service operations, and technology infrastructure to support continuous productivity.

Concept — Where Productivity Meets Specialty Coffee

The goal of “Việc” is to create a 24/7 workspace environment that is both comfortable and affordable. Instead of operating as a traditional cafe, the venue is structured as a hybrid productivity hub designed specifically for remote workers.

  • Affordable Quality: The beverage menu balances classic coffee, matcha drinks, and fresh juices with prices ranging from 25,000 – 60,000 VND.
  • Optimized Workspace: The three-floor, 120 m² facility dedicates approximately 80% of the space to individual workstations and 20% to private group rooms.
  • Uninterrupted Productivity: The facility provides enterprise-level WiFi (minimum 30 Mbps supporting 50 users), abundant power outlets, and sound-controlled quiet zones.

Operational Architecture

The cafe operates as an integrated service system composed of ten interconnected subsystems, covering food production, workspace management, customer service, security, and digital infrastructure.

  • Integrated POS System: A central Point-of-Sale platform manages beverage orders, workspace reservations, hourly room rentals, and inventory tracking.
  • Flexible Workspace Packages: Customers can book spaces in blocks of 2, 4, 6 hours or overnight sessions.
  • Service Level Agreements (SLA): Drinks must be served within 7 minutes of ordering, while room turnover and cleaning must be completed within 15 minutes.
  • Operational Continuity: A 2KVA backup generator ensures WiFi and POS systems remain operational during power outages.

Financial Blueprint

The system was designed with strong financial feasibility while maintaining affordability for students.

  • Initial Investment: Approximately 2 billion VND required for space renovation, equipment, and operational setup.
  • Revenue Target: Over 500 million VND monthly revenue through beverage sales and workspace rentals.
  • Net Profit: Estimated 100 – 125 million VND per month.
  • Return on Investment: Expected full capital recovery within 30 months with a 70% occupancy rate.

Risk Management Strategy

To ensure long-term sustainability, the project incorporates a detailed risk management framework.

  • Low Occupancy Risk: Addressed through targeted digital marketing and flexible pricing strategies.
  • Supply Chain Stability: Managed through a FIFO inventory system and backup suppliers for key ingredients such as coffee beans and milk.
  • Operational Disruptions: Mitigated with clear service protocols and backup power infrastructure.

Conclusion

“Việc” demonstrates how systems engineering principles can transform a traditional cafe into a fully integrated productivity ecosystem. By aligning workspace design, service operations, and digital infrastructure, the model offers a scalable blueprint for modern co-working environments serving the evolving needs of the gig economy.
