Chapter II

Translate the service model into floor layouts

The 3-floor layout turns the co-working cafe concept into a usable operating system: public-facing service on the ground floor, mixed seating and group capacity on the first floor, and quieter focus capacity on the second floor.

Ground floor layout

The ground floor handles arrival, ordering, and public seating. It places the entrance, service counter, open cafe tables, wall seating, restrooms, and support rooms together so first-time users can enter, order, sit, and move through the space with minimal confusion.

First floor layout

The first floor expands the workspace layer with shared tables, perimeter individual seats, a pantry/service point, restrooms, and a separated group-study room. This supports the 80/20 workspace split while giving teams a defined area for discussion.

Second floor layout

The second floor is designed as the quietest work zone, with more open study tables, individual wall seating, and a compact service corner. It increases usable focus capacity without adding the busier arrival and ordering flow from the ground floor.

Chapter III

Build the operating model

Allocate Workspace

Shape the cafe around work first, dining second. The plan prioritizes quiet seats, predictable access, and affordable work blocks.

  • Spatial Ratio: Dedicated 80% of the floor plan strictly to individual workstations, reserving 20% for private, acoustically treated group rooms.
  • Affordable Quality: Formulated a pricing matrix between 25,000 – 60,000 VND to remain highly competitive within the student demographic.
  • Flexible Packages: Integrated block-booking models allowing users to secure spaces in 2, 4, 6-hour chunks or dedicated overnight sessions.

Integrate Subsystems

Link beverage production, room booking, cleaning, payment, WiFi, and power backup through one service workflow.

  • Service Level Agreements (SLA): Mandated operational parameters requiring beverage delivery within 7 minutes and full room turnover/cleaning within 15 minutes.
  • Network Resilience: Deployed enterprise-grade WiFi guaranteeing a minimum of 30 Mbps, optimized to concurrently support 50+ intensive users.
  • Disaster Recovery: Installed a 2KVA backup generator to safeguard POS, network integrity, and ambient lighting during local grid failures.
Chapter IV

Control revenue and risk

Validate the system financially, then defend the plan against supply, occupancy, and service continuity risks.

Stabilize Supply

Enforced a strict FIFO (First-In, First-Out) inventory system. Integrated secondary and tertiary backup suppliers for mission-critical consumables (coffee beans, specialized milks) to prevent menu outages.

Protect Occupancy

Low occupancy risks are aggressively countered through targeted digital marketing in local university forums and dynamic, time-based pricing strategies to fill off-peak hours.

Conclusion

“Việc” demonstrates how systems engineering principles can transform a traditional cafe into a fully integrated productivity ecosystem. By aligning workspace design, service operations, and digital infrastructure, the model offers a highly scalable blueprint for modern co-working environments serving the evolving gig economy.
