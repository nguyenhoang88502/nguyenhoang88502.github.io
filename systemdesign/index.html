Executive Summary & System Specs

Engineered a comprehensive systems architecture for a 24/7 hybrid co-working cafe, targeting a high-density demographic of 200,000+ users in the National University Village. Architected a 3-floor, 120 m² facility with an 80/20 spatial allocation optimizing individual productivity and group collaboration. Deployed 10 interconnected operational subsystems, enforcing strict Service Level Agreements (SLAs) including 7-minute beverage delivery and 15-minute room turnover. Secured continuous operations via enterprise-grade 30 Mbps network infrastructure and 2KVA power redundancies. Formulated a high-yield financial model requiring 2 Billion VND in initial CAPEX, projecting 500 Million VND monthly revenue to achieve full ROI within 30 months at a 70% baseline occupancy.

Chapter II

Spatial & Operational Architecture

Workspace Optimization

The goal of “Việc” is to engineer a productivity hub tailored specifically for remote workers and the gig economy. The spatial design heavily favors deep work over transient dining.

  • Spatial Ratio: Dedicated 80% of the floor plan strictly to individual workstations, reserving 20% for private, acoustically treated group rooms.
  • Affordable Quality: Formulated a pricing matrix between 25,000 – 60,000 VND to remain highly competitive within the student demographic.
  • Flexible Packages: Integrated block-booking models allowing users to secure spaces in 2, 4, 6-hour chunks or dedicated overnight sessions.

Subsystem Integration

The facility operates as a centralized service system linking food production, workspace administration, and digital infrastructure via a unified Point-of-Sale (POS) platform.

  • Service Level Agreements (SLA): Mandated operational parameters requiring beverage delivery within 7 minutes and full room turnover/cleaning within 15 minutes.
  • Network Resilience: Deployed enterprise-grade WiFi guaranteeing a minimum of 30 Mbps, optimized to concurrently support 50+ intensive users.
  • Disaster Recovery: Installed a 2KVA backup generator to safeguard POS, network integrity, and ambient lighting during local grid failures.

Financial Blueprint & Risk Management

The system was designed with rigorous financial feasibility, ensuring long-term sustainability while defending against supply chain and operational risks.

Supply Chain Stability

Enforced a strict FIFO (First-In, First-Out) inventory system. Integrated secondary and tertiary backup suppliers for mission-critical consumables (coffee beans, specialized milks) to prevent menu outages.

Occupancy Defense

Low occupancy risks are aggressively countered through targeted digital marketing in local university forums and dynamic, time-based pricing strategies to fill off-peak hours.

Conclusion

“Việc” demonstrates how systems engineering principles can transform a traditional cafe into a fully integrated productivity ecosystem. By aligning workspace design, service operations, and digital infrastructure, the model offers a highly scalable blueprint for modern co-working environments serving the evolving gig economy.
