In complex business environments, choosing the optimal strategy requires balancing technical data, business priorities, and expert judgement. This case study explores the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), a structured multi-criteria decision framework, through the strategic planning of the EVLotus electric vehicle project.

What is the Analytic Hierarchy Process?

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured decision-making framework used to analyze complex problems involving multiple criteria. It breaks a large decision into smaller comparisons, allowing decision makers to quantify subjective judgments and determine the relative importance of each factor. Through weighted matrices and consistency checks, AHP produces decisions that are both logical and mathematically consistent.

Why Use AHP?

  • Objectivity: converts qualitative judgement into measurable data.
  • Consistency: the consistency ratio (CR) ensures logical comparisons.
  • Clarity: large strategic problems are broken into manageable decisions.

The EVLotus case demonstrates that strategic planning rarely involves a perfect choice. Instead, success lies in identifying the optimal solution based on clearly defined priorities. The Analytic Hierarchy Process provides a systematic roadmap for navigating complex decisions with transparency and analytical rigor.
